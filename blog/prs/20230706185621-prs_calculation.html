1. Intro

Polygenic Risk Score (PRS) is a measure of additive effects of Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs) . It allows using summary statistics of a large Genome Wide Association (GWAS) study.

(Collister, Liu, and Clifton 2022)

\begin{equation} PRS_j = \sum_{i}^{N} \beta_i \times dosage_{ij} \end{equation}

data$Affected <- as.factor(with(data, reorder(Affected, Total)))
data$TotalShifted <- data$Total - mean(data[which(data$Affected=="Normal"),"Total"])
print(head(data))

We shifted the total values by substracting the mean of the normals. 

ggplot(data, aes(x=TotalShifted, color=Affected, fill=Affected)) +
  geom_histogram(position="identity", alpha=.5) +
  geom_density(aes(y=1.2*..count..), alpha=0.2) +
  xlim(-5,6) +
  ylim(0,5)

When you visit www.pgscatalog.org or image search “Polygenic risk score” you would be seeing plot of these two peaks. Affected samples have more effect alleles with higher totals on average this is why their plot has slightly higher peak and larger mean.

  • Because most of the totals are the same they overlap and we plot only a few points as a result. position_jitter with w=0.5 spreads the points around a bit allowing us the plot all of the points.
ggplot(data, aes(x=Total, y=Affected, color=Affected)) +
  geom_point(position = position_jitter(w = 0.5, h = 0)) +
  scale_y_discrete(name ="Affected status", limits=c("Normal","Affected"))
